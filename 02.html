Root causes are the reasons to build the tool (2/2)

Problem 04

Unclear Ownership

There is no clearly defined "owner" for specific tools. Owners lack a visible identity accessible to the end-users. When something breaks, nobody knows who to call.

Consequence Extended Mean Time To Repair (MTTR) + Team downtime waiting for solutions + Blame shifting = LOSS OF MONEY.
WHY: Missing clear ownership and direct contact points. Users cannot immediately identify the guarantor of functionality.
Problem 05

High Entry Friction

Moving between devices forces users to re-verify identity, store passwords, and learn new environments. This leads to frustration and depleted cognitive energy.

Consequence Broken "Flow" state + Mental fatigue from barriers + Reluctance to use tools = LOSS OF MONEY.
WHY: Missing "Single Identity" principles.
Solution: Remove password barriers (Federated Identity) and ensure visual consistency.
Problem 06

Context Switching Penalty

The human brain cannot multitask; it only switches contexts. Every switch between disparate tools incurs a "re-focusing tax" (approx. 23 mins to regain deep focus).

Consequence Fragmented attention span + Increased error rate + Burnout risk + Shallow work = LOSS OF MONEY.
WHY: Biological limitation. The brain craves continuity. Tool fragmentation fights against human neurobiology.
