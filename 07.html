Understanding the Users

Answering the question: "Who is it for?"
  • The user is the one who spends the most time and effort working with the tool. Their efficiency multiplied by the number of users = HIGHEST PRIORITY AND ROI.
  • Designing from "backend forward" does not work. Backend designers/developers are not the end-users who request specific UX/UI. Their technical worldview often clashes with the mental models and needs of the general employee base.
  • Management's Vision vs. Reality "In the Trenches". If management is disconnected from the practical daily reality of workers, their ideas about tool functionality often create friction and unusable processes.
(Scalability Principle): Potential for implementation across various entities: The tool must be prepared for thousands of collaborators across different countries. Diverse "data sets," quantities, roles, and languages require a universal axiomatic foundation, rather than a solution "tailor-made" for a single group.
